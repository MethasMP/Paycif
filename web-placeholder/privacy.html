Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 2026

1. Introduction

Paycif ("we", "our", or "us"), operated by Methas Pakpoompong, is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy details how we collect, use, and share your personal information when you use the Paycif platform, application, and related services.

2. Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide and improve our services:

  • Personal Identification Data: Name, email address, phone number, and identity verification documents required for KYC/AML compliance.
  • Transaction Data: Payment details, PromptPay destination data, transaction volume, and timestamps. Note: We DO NOT store raw credit card numbers; we utilize PCI-compliant third-party processors.
  • Technical Data: IP addresses, device identifiers, browser types, and usage analytics.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • To execute and settle transactions accurately.
  • To verify your identity and comply with global Anti-Money Laundering (AML) laws.
  • To detect, prevent, and mitigate fraud and technical issues.
  • To improve our application interface and provide timely customer support.

4. How We Share Your Information

We may share your data with trusted entities under strict confidentiality agreements:

  • Payment Processors & Gateways: Authorized Tier-1 liquidity and payment providers, for the sole purpose of authorizing and settling transactions.
  • Regulatory Bodies: When legally required by law enforcement or financial intelligence units to comply with AML/CTF obligations.

5. Data Security & Storage

We maintain industry-standard security measures, including AES-256 encryption for data at rest and TLS 1.3 for data in transit. Despite our rigorous efforts, no method of electronic transmission is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Your Data Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your personal data. Because financial records must be kept for regulatory compliance, some transaction data may be retained for up to 5-10 years as required by local laws.

7. PDPA Compliance (Thailand)

For users in Thailand, we comply with the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA). You have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your personal data.
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of data, subject to financial record retention laws.
  • Right to Portability: Request transmission of your data to another provider in a common format.
  • Right to Object: Object to processing for marketing or non-essential purposes.
